What's in The Garden

In Our Community Garden Project, we have a variety of features and areas that contribute to a vibrant and productive gardening space. Here's a glimpse of what you can find in our garden.

Polytunnel

Our garden is equipped with a polytunnel, a structure covered with a specialized greenhouse film that helps create a controlled environment for plant growth. The polytunnel offers protection from harsh weather conditions, extends the growing season, and provides ideal conditions for cultivating a wide range of plants. It's a versatile space where we grow various crops, including tender vegetables, herbs, and flowers.

Greenhouses

In addition to the polytunnel, we have a series of greenhouses that allow us to create optimal growing conditions for specific plants. These greenhouses provide controlled temperature, humidity, and light levels, making them ideal for starting seedlings, nurturing delicate plants, and extending the growing season for heat-loving crops. Inside the greenhouses, you'll find a diverse array of plants thriving under carefully managed environments.

Raised Beds

The garden features raised beds, which are elevated planting areas built with wooden or stone borders. These beds offer several advantages, including improved drainage, better soil quality control, and easier access for planting, maintenance, and harvesting. We have a variety of raised beds in different sizes and configurations, providing opportunities for members to grow their preferred crops.

Fresh Produce

At Our Community Garden Project, we take pride in growing a wide range of fresh produce. Our garden is home to an abundant selection of vegetables, herbs, fruits, and flowers. Some of the popular crops we cultivate include tomatoes, potatoes, radishes, lettuce, carrots, cucumbers, peppers, strawberries, and much more. We prioritize organic and sustainable growing practices, ensuring that the produce is healthy, nutritious, and free from harmful chemicals.

Summary

In summary, our garden encompasses a polytunnel and greenhouses for controlled growing environments, as well as raised beds for convenient gardening. With a focus on fresh produce, we cultivate a diverse range of crops, including tomatoes, potatoes, radishes, and many other vegetables, herbs, and fruits. This variety allows our members to enjoy a bountiful harvest and experience the joy of growing their own food.

Additionally, the project has access to the adjacent Chester Community Center for meetings, activities, refreshments, and conveniences. Additionally, we have plans to construct our own converted shipping container outbuilding in the near future. This facility will serve as a dedicated space for our project, providing further amenities and meeting space for our members.
